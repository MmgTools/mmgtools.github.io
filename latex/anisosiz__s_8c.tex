Define size at points by intersecting the surfacic metric and the physical metric.

The output metric is\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item at singular points\+: isotropic
\item at ridge points\+: anisotropic in the orthonormal basis defined by the tangent at the ridge and the normal at each portion of surface.
\item at surface boundary points\+: anisotropic in an orthonormal basis difined in the tangent plane and the direction normal to this plane.
\end{DoxyItemize}

\begin{DoxyWarning}{Warning}
What we are doing on non-\/manifold points has to be improved\+: as such points are marked as MG\+\_\+\+CRN and MG\+\_\+\+REQ, we first try to call \mbox{\hyperlink{anisosiz__3d_8c_a6bb3f73ec3910c7b3bcbf6c0670059c9}{MMG5\+\_\+defmetsin}} that likely fails (because \mbox{\hyperlink{boulep_8c_a71f8e5d4eb5962634fdd646773a14e09}{MMG5\+\_\+boulet}} don\textquotesingle{}t work for non-\/manifod points due to the missing of consistent adjacencies relationships), then we call \mbox{\hyperlink{anisosiz_8c_a8cbf7130be23d609bcc515b0c4c5351a}{MMG5\+\_\+def\+Uninit\+Size}} and we set hmax on non-\/manifold points. Note that the building of adjacency table depends on the initial mesh numbering, thus, in certain cases, MMG5\+\_\+boulet will succeed... 
\end{DoxyWarning}
Step 1\+: Set metric at points belonging to a required edge\+: compute the metric as the mean of the length of the required eges passing through the point

Step 2\+: Travel all the points (via triangles) in the mesh and set metric tensor

search for unintialized metric

Remark\+: as non manifold points are marked as CRN and REQ, we first try to call defmetsin that fails (because MMG5\+\_\+boulet don\textquotesingle{}t work for non-\/manifod points), then we pass here and we set hmax on non-\/manifold points
